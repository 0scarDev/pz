Overview

Getting more sleep has been something that I've been obsessed over for the last 5 years. I used to get around 4 hours of sleep each night, go to class, fall asleep during lecture and then shuffle along until the next day for 2 years. I realized that getting only 4 hours of sleep a night but then living the next 20 hours on 50% was actually a net loss in consciousness. I'd rather approach the lifestyle where I slept for 8 and had 100% consciousness for the other 16 hours in the day. So I went on a massive binge on science journals and nearly exhausted every resource on the internet about sleep hygiene. One thing that I had no control over that had a significant impact on sleep hygiene was the sun. Manipulating sunlight is our project’s main selling point.

 

Approach

Spending long periods of time in bed isn't a great indicator of how good one sleeps. For example if someone spends 8 hours in bed but only sleeps for 6 hours, they have a 75% efficiency. The goal of this project is to push this efficiency to approach 100% by optimizing the determinants that we cannot control.

 

In sleep science there is a term called zeitbergers (PZ = project zeitberger), which are parameters that significantly affect every human's sleep/wake cycle, popularly known as the circadian rhythm. This device will allow humans to optimize the biggest, most significant factor in our sleep/wake cycle, light.

 

When we go to sleep at night we all face a choice: to close or not close the blinds. Both choices have their advantages and disadvantages. Closing the blinds blocks all the ambient light that seeps into our bedrooms at night, which is crucial for falling asleep quickly but does nothing for helping us get up in the morning since we have no natural light. Opening the blinds lets in sunlight in the morning, which is crucial for waking up quickly and well rested in the morning but does nothing for helping us fall asleep at night since ambient light will still be present. We normally cannot have both positive sides.

 

My project will combine the best of both options in an automated, smart-phone app driven, alarm-clock or Google Calendar integrated, and bluetooth/wifi enabled machine (an internet-of-things project). The machine will be lightweight since the display, logic, and memory are all hosted on the phone. The machine should be modular, to be compatible with all possible blinds: twisters, horizontal sliders, vertical sliders, and whatever else may come about (since we only have 10 weeks, we could just implement the most popular mechanic: the vertical twisty twisty. The complementary app will be have features that allow blinds to be closed half way for mood lighting or close slowly over the course of x hours, and more. Remember this project isn't just for improving the sleep hygiene of those with smartphones. This could also be implemented in hospitals in classrooms to automatically dim the blinds, or in office buildings after closing hours.

Constraints, Risk and Feasibility

 We ultimately want to integrate our software with our custom made hardware since Arduino has a bit of overkill for what we are trying to do. We want to make our hardware as small as possible so that the cost of making a case, making the microcontroller, shipping, power consumption, and paying royalties will be minimized.

No one has real experience integrating mobile apps with Arduino, so we might get stuck multiple times which would severely slow down our development process.

What is realistically feasible here? We should be able to develop something that can be kickstarted. It may not be fully what we want in the end. But the main benefits of our device can be reaped from the final presentation of our project. Of course, the rest of the features we add will be quality-of-life add ons.

 The main risk is that we don’t work at a fast enough pace to implement what we promised at the beginning of the quarter. We could be divided and communication between people can be slow. Since we only have 2 people on each team, it could be hard to move at a face pace as the hardware and software team depend on each other. However, since we have only 2 on each part of the project, I’d want for the software and hardware team to meet frequently to take advantage of the fact that 2 schedules line up more 4. Of course, we would still have 4 person meetings.

Group Management

We will have a software team and a hardware team, both composed of two people. There will be no software lead or hardware lead.

Decisions will be made by majority vote, with tie-breakers depending on the project leader’s vote. This allows for everybody’s voice to be evenly weighted until the event of a tie.

We primarily communicate through facebook. Tasks are organized in Trello. In person group meetings occur once every week while the two teams meet by themselves at their discretion.

We have specific deadlines, and we work on upcoming deadlines if the ones right now have already been done. This should make it it easy for us to deal with schedule slips. We have access to tutors in the CSE department as well as the ECE department if we really can’t do it solely by ourselves.

The software team, Rui-hao Oscar Qiu and Alvin Yan, will be responsible for the software side milestones, and Pedro Coutin and and Guan Diego Wong will do similarly for hardware.

The project lead, Rui-hao Oscar Qiu will generate weekly logistical status reports.
